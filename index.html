Our mission

  • Position tracking using a GNSS module and ceramic antenna.
  • Acceleration and rotation measurement with a gyroscope and IMU.
  • Data logging via an SD card.
  • Payload area for additional secondary missions and extra sensors.
  • Optimized microcontroller selection with a precisely chosen 32-bit MCU.
  • Use of sodium-ion batteries for improved power efficiency.
  • Code optimization with FreeRTOS and enhanced error handling.
  • Modular design with interchangeable satellite covers (one with a camera, one without) and a flexible payload area for various missions.
  • This setup ensures adaptability, efficiency, and improved data collection in our secondary mission.

Cansat competition

The CanSat competition is about designing, building, testing, and launching a simulated satellite. This satellite must strictly adhere to key rules, including specific weight, height, width, and depth limits, while also staying within a tight budget.

The satellite's mandatory base mission is to measure altitude and temperature and transmit this data to the ground station. Each team can have a maximum of six members, making teamwork an essential part of the challenge.
